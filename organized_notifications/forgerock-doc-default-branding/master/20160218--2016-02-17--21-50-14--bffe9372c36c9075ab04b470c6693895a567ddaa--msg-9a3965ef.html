bffe9372c36 DOCS-230 PDF: Avoid bad line breaks in the middle of literals

This patch limits hyphenation in `<literal>` elements to occur
only after the characters, `.`, `/`, `,`, and `-`,
and adopts hyphenation for URLs as described in Bob Stanton's book.

The example problems in the description could be avoided
by using more specific markup, as shown in the following examples:

`Use the <option>--defaultAdd</option> option to treat records with no changetype as add operations.`

`To remove our software, use the <command>rm -rf /path/to/oursoftware</command> command.`

Notice the use of `<option>` and `<command>` elements, rather than `<literal>` in these cases.		 17 Feb 2016
